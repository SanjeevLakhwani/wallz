# Wallz — App Concept

## Elevator Pitch

Wallz is a social discovery app built around physical fiducial markers placed in the real world. Find a tag, scan it, save it forever — but each tag only lives 30 days, and you only get to discover it once.

---

## What Are Fiducial Markers?

Fiducial markers are machine-readable visual tags (think QR codes, but custom-designed). In Wallz, every marker is **generated by the app** — unique, verifiable, and tied to a specific real-world location and creator. Users print or display them and place them on physical objects, walls, or spaces in the world.

---

## Core Mechanics

### Discovery (The Hunt)
- The map shows **regions** with a count of active markers nearby — not exact pin locations.
- Users explore the real world to physically find markers.
- Once found and scanned: the tag is **permanently saved** to your profile.
- Each tag can only be discovered by any given user **once, ever** — no replays.

### Tag Lifecycle
- Every tag is **active for 30 days** from the moment it goes live.
- After 30 days, the tag **expires** — it can no longer be discovered by new users.
- Already-saved tags remain in your collection permanently, even after expiry.
- This creates urgency: find it before it's gone.

### Submission & Approval
1. Creator uploads a photo/scan of the placed marker in the real world.
2. Submission includes a general location (neighborhood / area level).
3. Moderation team **approves** the submission for authenticity and appropriateness.
4. Approved tag goes live on the map — the 30-day clock starts.

---

## Social Layer

### On Each Tag
- **Likes** — react to a tag you discovered.
- **Views** — passive count of how many users scanned/viewed it.
- **Comments** — threaded discussion on any tag.
- **Save count** — how many people saved it before it expired.

### Map Search & Filters
Users can filter the map by:
- **Most liked** — highest community appreciation
- **Most viewed** — highest traffic
- **Most recent** — newly approved tags
- **Expiring soon** — tags with <7 days left (hidden gem urgency)

---

## Key Design Principles

| Principle | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| No pinpoint location | Preserves the physical hunt; makes discovery feel earned |
| 30-day expiry | Creates scarcity and urgency; rewards active explorers |
| One-time discovery | Makes each find meaningful; no farming |
| Permanent saves | Your collection is a real record of places you've been |
| Approval gate | Keeps content quality high and locations authentic |

## Tech Considerations

- **Fiducial marker generation**: Custom marker library (e.g. ArUco-style or proprietary)
- **Marker verification**: App validates scanned marker matches database record
- **Location privacy**: Store only approximate area (bounding box / geohash), never exact coords
- **Map**: Clustered region view (e.g. heatmap or count bubbles by geohash cell)
- **Expiry jobs**: Scheduled background task to deactivate tags at 30-day mark
- **Moderation queue**: Internal dashboard for approving/rejecting submissions

---

## What Makes This Different

Most geo-social apps (Snapchat Snap Map, Google Maps contributions) show you exactly where things are. Wallz removes the pin — **you have to actually go look**. The tag is the destination, not a pointer to one. Combined with expiry and one-time discovery, every find is a genuine moment.
